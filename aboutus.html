About Us

The Computer Science Department is one of the 11 that commenced activities in the 2012/2013 academic session with the admission of forty-two (42) students; nine (9) academic staff and one (1) non-academic staff, with Professor Sunday Eric Adewumi as the pioneer Head of Department. The initial academic programme adopted the NUC Minimum Academic Benchmark but was subsequently reviewed in 2014 and 2018 to accommodate present realities and key into the grand challenges in Computer Science.

After the graduation of the first set of students in 2016, the Department was ripe for the commencement of higher degrees. Consequently, during the 2019/2020 Academic Session, the Department welcomed the first set of students into the Postgraduate Diploma (PGD), Master of Science (MSc), and Doctor of Philosophy (PhD) in Computer Science. The Department has a well-equipped computer laboratory furnished and equipped Mathematics laboratory for practical applications in solving real problems for both undergraduate and postgraduate students.

Vision

To be the most outstanding Computer Science department among the nine newly established Federal Universities in Nigeria in 2011 and one of the top-ranking academic program departments in Africa.

Mission Statement

To produce graduates that are able to develop and deploy cutting-edge applications geared towards solving societal challenges.

Philosophy

The program aims to provide students with a broad and balanced foundation of Computer Science knowledge and skills, enabling them to apply their knowledge for solving theoretical and practical problems in Computer Science and contribute to the development of relevant ICT for national progress. Students are also encouraged to pursue further studies in Computer Science and multi-disciplinary areas related to Computer Science. The program equips students with a range of applicable skills in information technology that can be utilized in various aspects of human endeavors. Additionally, students develop general skills in non-subject-specific competencies, ICT capacity, communication, interpersonal, and organization skills.
